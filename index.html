Hi, I'm Joy Lita

Most corporate training fails because it treats employees like students who need to be lectured. I don’t believe in lecturing. I am a Learning Experience Designer who believes that real competence comes from doing, not listening. I specialize in taking the complex operational procedures found in industries like Finance, Logistics, and Hospitality and turning them into practical, hands-on practice environments. I don't obsess over abstract learning theories; I obsess over KPIs. My goal is to design the simulations and real-world experiences that get your team performing at speed in five days instead of twenty. If the employees aren't performing better on the job, the training hasn't done its work. I bridge the gap between academic design and the reality of getting work done.

Portfolio Projects

Project 1: Enterprise Curriculum Architecture

Curriculum ArchitectureTNABlended Learning

Training shouldn't just be a checklist; it should solve a business problem. When a global enterprise client needed to slash onboarding time, I didn't just shorten the slides. I performed a Training Needs Analysis to identify exactly what was causing the performance gap.

I cut out the "nice-to-know" fluff and built a 6-day "Proficiency Track." We shifted the evaluation from passive quizzes to a rigorous Pass/Fail gate for critical safety and financial protocols. The result was a team that hit the floor ready to work, not just ready to recite company history.

Project 2: Interactive Storyboarding

Articulate 360Scenario-Based Learning

E-learning is often boring because it ignores how adults actually learn. For this project, I developed a story-driven module that focused on the guest journey. Instead of listing policies, I built branching scenarios using the L.A.S.T. recovery model, forcing learners to make decisions that mirror real-world guest interactions.

The core of this design was "Persona Matching"—a drag-and-drop activity that trains staff to decode guest intent before they even speak. If the training doesn't prepare you for the chaotic reality of a real shift, it’s not effective design.

Project 3: "TP Air" – Immersive Aviation Simulation

Unreal Engine3D SimulationGamification

Video is passive; simulation is active. To master complex safety protocols, I built a 3D environment where learners navigate a cabin to complete specific operational tasks. By interacting with the environment—closing compartments, securing windows, and locating seats—learners build muscle memory in a risk-free space. If you want high retention, you have to let the learner experience the environment, not just look at it.
